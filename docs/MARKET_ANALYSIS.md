## Differentiators

- **Unified platform** across 6 suites — most competitors are point solutions in one domain
- **AI governance baked in** — every AI model is registered, versioned, explainable, and audited. This is ahead of where most GRC vendors are
- **Multi-tenant SaaS-ready** — full RLS, tenant provisioning pipeline, per-tenant rate limiting
- **vCISO with LLM + predictive** — conversational AI for security with grounded responses, not just a chatbot wrapper
- **Workflow engine** — cross-suite automation with visual designer

## Valuation Benchmarks

The codebase represents significant engineering effort — a full-stack, multi-service, multi-database enterprise SaaS platform. Market worth depends on factors beyond the code:

- **Revenue / ARR** — the single biggest driver of SaaS valuation
- **Customers & contracts** — proof of product-market fit
- **Team** — investors value the team as much as the tech
- **Go-to-market** — sales, marketing, partnerships
- **Competitive moat** — patents, data network effects, switching costs

### As Pure IP / Technology

Comparable early-stage enterprise security+GRC platforms have raised or been valued at:

| Stage | Typical Valuation |
|-------|-------------------|
| Pre-revenue / MVP | $2M – $10M |
| Early traction (few customers, <$1M ARR) | $10M – $30M |
| Growth ($1M – $5M ARR) | $30M – $100M+ (15–25x ARR) |

The breadth of what's built (6 integrated suites, AI governance, workflow engine, full frontend) is unusual for an early-stage product — that's either a strength (platform play) or a risk (too broad, not deep enough in any one market) depending on the go-to-market strategy.

## Bottom Line

The technology is substantial. The market value depends entirely on what happens next — customers, revenue, and team.
